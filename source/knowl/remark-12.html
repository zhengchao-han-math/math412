Remark 1.7.2.

Stone's generalization of Weierstrass Theorem is insightful, exploiting the algebraic structure in the approximation. It applies in fairly general settings. Its proof is more suitable for quiet study than a classroom presentation. Due to time limitation, we will not spend classroom time on its proof. I advice you to carefully digest the argument in steps 3 and 4 of Rudin's proof, and understand where the continuity and compactness play crucial roles.
