Our formulation allows us to study \(\bff (\bx, \by)=\mathbf 0\) when \(\bff\) may not be differentiable in \(\by\text{.}\) E.g., \(f(x, y)=x |y|+e^{x}\) is differentiable in \(x\text{,}\) \(f(0, 0)=1\text{,}\) \(D_{x}f(x, y)=|y|+e^{x}\) is continuous with \(D_{x}f(0, 0)=1\text{,}\) so Theorem 4.5.8 allows us to conclude that there exists a continuous \(x=g(y)\) for \(y\) near \(0\) with \(g(0)=0\) such that \(|y|g(y)+e^{g(y)}=1\text{.}\)
