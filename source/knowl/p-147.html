Without discussing details of Lebesgue integrable functions, we may assume that there exists some \(\hat g\in L^{2}[-l, l]\) such that \(\int_{-l}^{l} |\hat g-S_{N}[g](x)|^{2}\, dx\to 0\) as \(N\to \infty\text{.}\) We claim the following property:
\begin{equation*} (g-\hat g, e^{\frac{i mx}{l}})=0 \quad \text{for any $m\in \bbZ$.} \end{equation*}
This follows by noting that for any \(N\gt m\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} (g-S_{N}[g](x), e^{\frac{i mx}{l}})=(g, e^{\frac{i mx}{l}})-(S_{N}[g](x), e^{\frac{i mx}{l}})=2l\left(c_{m}-c_{m}\right)=0, \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} (g-\hat g, e^{\frac{i mx}{l}})=(g-S_{N}[g](x), e^{\frac{i mx}{l}})+(S_{N}[g](x)-\hat g, e^{\frac{i mx}{l}}) = (S_{N}[g](x)-\hat g, e^{\frac{i mx}{l}}), \end{equation*}
while by the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality
\begin{equation*} \vert (S_{N}[g](x)-\hat g, e^{\frac{i mx}{l}}) \vert \le \Vert S_{N}[g](x)-\hat g\Vert \Vert e^{\frac{i mx}{l}}\Vert \to 0 \end{equation*}
as \(N\to \infty\text{,}\) which shows that
\begin{equation*} (g-\hat g, e^{\frac{i mx}{l}})= 0. \end{equation*}
