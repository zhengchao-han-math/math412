Note also that if we use \(r^{-1}\frac{\partial f }{\partial \theta}\) instead of \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial \theta}\) in the relation above we would get a simpler relation using an orthogonal matrix:
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f}{\partial r} & r^{-1} \frac{\partial f}{\partial \theta} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & - \sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
