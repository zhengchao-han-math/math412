If the most elementary notion of point-wise convergence is used in (3.0.1), then it is not so easy to justify the interchange of summation and integration. It turns out that a more useful notion of convergence in this context is that of mean square convergence defined as
\begin{equation*} \lim_{N\to \infty} \int_{0}^{l}\vert g(x) - \sum_{n=1}^{N} c_n \sin \left(\frac{n \pi x}{l} \right)\vert^{2} \, dx \to 0. \end{equation*}
